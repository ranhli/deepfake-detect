Detecting AI-generated Text Including Code

Overview of Research

Large language models (LLMs) are highly effective in generating text for tasks like question answering and code generation. However, their widespread use introduces risks such as cybersecurity threats, the spread of misinformation, and academic dishonesty. They can also degrade software security and reduce the quality of training data for foundational models. Therefore, detecting and auditing machine-generated text is essential to address these challenges.

Columbia Researchers have developed an innovative system named Raidar (geneRative AI Detection viA Rewriting). This multi modular deepfake detection tool determines whether text, audio has been constructed by a human or generated by AI or large language models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, without the need for access to the model’s internal workings.

Ethics and Disclosure

The Raidar system (geneRative AI Detection viA Rewriting) developed by Columbia Researchers is provided for academic and research purposes only. The primary intent of this research is to advance knowledge in the field of AI-generated text detection and to foster further academic inquiry and development.
